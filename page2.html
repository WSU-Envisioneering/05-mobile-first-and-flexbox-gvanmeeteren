Coco Gauff

Nationality: United States

Born: March 13, 2004 (Atlanta, GA, USA)

Playing Style: Right-handed with a two-handed backhand; blends power, speed, and mental grit.

Career Highlights & Rankings: Turned pro in 2018 and burst onto the scene by reaching Wimbledon's main draw at age 15, where she famously defeated Venus Williams. She is the current world No. 2 in singles, with a career-high of No. 2 achieved in June 2024. Also reached World No. 1 in doubles in August 2022.

Grand Slam Titles: 2023 US Open (singles), 2025 French Open (singles), 2024 French Open (doubles)


Carlos Alcaraz

Nationality: Spain

Born: May 5, 2003 (El Palmar, Murcia, Spain)

Playing Style: Right-handed with a two-handed backhand. Uses an aggressive, all-court style combining power, quick movement, high tennis IQ, and mental resilience on all surfaces.

Career Highlights & Rankings: Turned pro in 2018 at age 15 and became the youngest male in Open Era to be ranked world No. 1 on September 12, 2022.

Grand Slam Titles: 2022 US Open, 2023 Wimbledon, 2024 French Open, 2024 Wimbledon, 2025 French Open (all singles)


Aryna Sabalenka

Nationality: Belarus

Born: May 5, 1998 (Minsk, Belarus)

Playing Style: Right-handed with a two-handed backhand; known for raw power, a dominant serve, and aggressive baseline play.

Career Highlights & Rankings: Turned pro in 2015, and reached world No. 1 in singles in September 2023. She reached current world No. 1 in singles as of August 2025.

Grand Slam Titles: 2023 Australian Open (singles), 2024 Australian Open (singles), 2024 US Open (singles), 2019 US Open (doubles), 2021 Australian Open (doubles)


Novak Djokovic

Nationality: Serbia

Born: May 22, 1987 (Belgrade, Yugoslavia)

Playing Style: Right-handed with a two-handed backhand; known for exceptional flexibility, defensive skills, and all-court versatility. Combines relentless cosistency, elite movement and mental toughness to outlast opponents in long rallies.

Career Highlights & Rankings: Turned pro in 2003 and first reached world No. 1 in singles in July 2011. Holds record for most weeks as world No. 1 (428 weeks) and most year-end No. 1 rankings (8 times). Olympic gold medalist in men's singles at the 2024 Paris Olympics.

Grand Slam Titles: Australian Open (10 in total), French Open (3), Wimbledon (7), US Open (4) (all singles titles), 2008 Australian Open (doubles)
